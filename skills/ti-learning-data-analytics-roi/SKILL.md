# Thought Industries — data, analytics & business impact

## Purpose

Align analytics conversations with Thought Industries’ public positioning: measure **site**, **content**, and **monetization** performance using **prebuilt reports** and connect learning data to **business systems** to assess **impact on key business metrics**.

## When to Use

- Defining KPIs for a TI program (customer success, marketing, or L&D stakeholders)
- Writing reporting requirements or integration specs (high level)
- Building an ROI story that links training to revenue, retention, or adoption

## Instruction areas

1. **Three lenses.** Structure metrics under: **platform / site health**, **content effectiveness**, **commerce & monetization** (when applicable).
2. **Prebuilt vs custom.** Start with what standard reports typically cover (engagement, progress, commerce—exact names depend on product version); layer custom questions the user needs for their board or QBR.
3. **Business system integration.** For “impact on key metrics,” require the user to name systems (CRM, CS platform, data warehouse) and the **direction of truth** (e.g. completion → health score). Do not invent field-level mappings without source docs.
4. **TI’s published KPI thinking.** TI promotes resources on **ROI of customer education** and **KPIs**—use as prompts to ask which outcomes matter (retention, expansion, support deflection, certification throughput, partner-sourced revenue, etc.).
5. **Privacy & governance.** Treat learner data per customer policy; flag PII minimization and access control when specifying exports or APIs.

## Example question bank (ask the user)

- Which **north-star** ties learning to revenue or retention this quarter?
- What **counterfactual** or baseline exists (cohorts, regions, product lines)?
- Who **consumes** the dashboard (exec, CS, partner manager)?
- What **actions** change when a metric moves (playbooks)?

## Quality checklist

- [ ] At least one business outcome beyond course completions
- [ ] Integration or export assumptions explicit or marked unknown
- [ ] Monetization metrics included when training is sold or bundled

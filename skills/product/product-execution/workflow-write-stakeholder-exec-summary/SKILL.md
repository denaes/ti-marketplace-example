# Stakeholder / exec summary

Produce a one-pager or short narrative for product or leadership: status, scope, risks, and next steps. Use when you need to communicate upward or across teams without sharing full backlog or PRD.

## When to Use

- You need a **one-pager** for leadership or a steering meeting
- You want a **stakeholder summary** (discovery, project, or product area) that is concise and actionable
- You are preparing for a **readiness** conversation (VP/CPO or Director) and want structured advice

## Prerequisites

- Input: context (project name, scope, current state, open questions, or link to PRD/epic)
- Skills: `skills/product/product-execution/discover-stakeholder-summary/SKILL.md`; optionally `skills/product/product-leadership/vp-cpo-readiness-advisor/SKILL.md`, `skills/product/product-leadership/director-readiness-advisor/SKILL.md`

## Step 1: Clarify audience and purpose

1. **Confirm with the user:** Who is the audience? (exec, product lead, cross-team) What is the ask? (status update, decision, resource, prioritization)
2. **Inputs:** Do they have a PRD, epic summary, or bullet list? If not, gather: what is the scope, what is done, what is at risk, what is needed next.
3. If they want **readiness** coaching (VP/CPO or Director transition), use the advisors in Step 3.

## Step 2: Read reference materials

1. **discover-stakeholder-summary** — `skills/product/product-execution/discover-stakeholder-summary/SKILL.md` and its EXAMPLE/TEMPLATE.
2. Use the template structure: objective, scope, status, risks/blockers, next steps, ask.

## Step 3: Draft the summary

1. Use **discover-stakeholder-summary** to produce a one-pager from the context they provided. Keep it to one page; use bullets and short sentences.
2. If the user asked for **readiness** support:
   - **VP/CPO:** Use **vp-cpo-readiness-advisor** for conversation flow and key messages.
   - **Director:** Use **director-readiness-advisor** for transition situations (preparing, interviewing, newly landed, recalibrating).

## Step 4: Output and next steps

1. Write the summary to a file (e.g. `workspace/_notes/YYYY-MM-DD_stakeholder-summary.md`).
2. Optionally offer a 30-second verbal version or talking points for a meeting.

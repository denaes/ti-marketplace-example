# Thought Industries — Customer Learning & Intelligence Platform (overview)

## Purpose

Ground answers in **Thought Industries’ public positioning** ([thoughtindustries.com](https://www.thoughtindustries.com/)): a **Customer Learning & Intelligence Platform** focused on **external audiences**—not only employees. The platform narrative emphasizes **creating, delivering, monetizing, and measuring** training across the customer and partner ecosystem.

## When to Use

- Drafting positioning, pitch decks, or internal briefs that must match TI’s marketed capabilities
- Explaining “what TI is” vs. a generic internal LMS
- Scoping initiatives that span **content, distribution, commerce, analytics, and integrations**

## Platform pillars (public site)

Use this structure when organizing narratives or roadmaps:

| Pillar | Public emphasis |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Learning experiences** | Dynamic dashboards, catalogs, learning paths; author and deliver content in-platform—from short video to comprehensive paths |
| **Personalization & distribution** | Right training to the right audience across customers, partners, members, prospects; tagging and flexible distribution; learner choice of what, how, and when |
| **Monetization** | Subscriptions, collections, bundles (courses and events); individuals, groups, organizations; license or purchase models |
| **Data & analytics** | Site, content, and monetization performance; prebuilt reports; connect platform data to business systems to tie learning to business metrics |
| **Integrate & extend** | Headless framework, webhooks, APIs; prebuilt and custom integrations; automate workflows across the stack |

## Instructions

1. **Lead with ecosystem language.** Prefer “customers, partners, members, prospects” over “employees only” unless the use case is explicitly employee L&D (which TI also lists as a use case).
2. **Avoid inventing features.** Stick to themes above; for product truth and APIs, defer to internal docs or codebase—not marketing copy alone.
3. **Cross-link skills.** For depth, use sibling skills under `skills/ti-skills/` (use cases, authoring, monetization, analytics, integrations).

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Describes create / deliver / monetize / measure for **external** audiences where relevant
- [ ] Mentions at least three of the five pillars above when describing “the platform”
- [ ] Does not claim specifics (SLAs, exact SKUs, roadmap) not supported by the user’s sources

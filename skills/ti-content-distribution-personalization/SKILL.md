# Thought Industries — content management, distribution & personalization

## Purpose

Encode Thought Industries’ public positioning on **personalization at scale**: deliver the **right training to the right audience** across a **network** of customers, partners, members, and **prospects**, using **smart tagging** and **flexible content distribution**, while respecting learner agency (what, how, when).

## When to Use

- Multi-tenant or multi-audience rollouts (e.g. different catalogs per customer or partner tier)
- Designing tag taxonomies, audiences, or content packages
- Explaining how TI-style personalization differs from one-size-fits-all course libraries

## Instructions

1. **Inventory audiences.** List each distinct audience (customer org, partner type, member tier, prospect pool). For each, capture: goals, restrictions, and what they must never see.
2. **Design tagging for behavior, not only labels.** Tags should drive **who sees what** and **recommended paths**; avoid tags that only exist for reporting unless reporting is the goal.
3. **Distribution rules.** Document rules in plain language: “If audience A → surfaces catalog X and path Y”; “If prospect → free modules Z only.” Tie rules to monetization where paywalled (see `ti-training-monetization-ecommerce`).
4. **Learner choice.** TI’s site emphasizes learners choosing what, how, and when—balance **guided programs** with **optional libraries** so narratives are credible.
5. **Multi-tenancy note.** Public monetization content references **Panorama** for licensing content across enterprise, distributor, or reseller contexts—use when describing B2B2x models; confirm architecture with internal sources.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] More than one audience explicitly considered when the program is not single-tenant
- [ ] Tagging or distribution rules are testable (“given segment S, user sees set C”)
- [ ] Prospects handled intentionally (included or explicitly out of scope)

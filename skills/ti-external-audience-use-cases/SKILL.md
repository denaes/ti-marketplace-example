# Thought Industries — external audience use cases

## Purpose

Classify initiatives using the **six use cases** called out on Thought Industries’ public site. Each has a distinct primary audience and business outcome; use this skill to **frame problems, metrics, and content strategy** consistently with TI’s marketing.

## When to Use

- Scoping a PRD, brief, or discovery doc and choosing the primary “motion” (customer vs partner vs member, etc.)
- Writing personas or ICPs for a TI-powered academy or learning site
- Comparing “education as marketing” vs “customer training” investment

## Use case reference (public site)

| Use case | Primary idea | Typical outcomes (as positioned) |
|----------|--------------|----------------------------------|
| **Customer training** | Train customers on your product or service | Growth, retention, revenue, customer value |
| **Partner enablement** | Train channel partners and distributors | Partners showcase your strengths; scaled enablement |
| **Member education** | Associations and professional communities | Recruit, engage, retain members |
| **Professional learning** | Credentialing / professional development | Learning tied to revenue and outcomes |
| **Education as marketing** | Pre-sales education | Attract and educate future customers on products and services |
| **Employee learning & development** | Enterprise training in one solution | Consolidated employee training (still within TI’s story) |

## Instructions

1. **Pick one primary use case** per initiative. Note secondary audiences (e.g. prospects benefiting from customer training content) in assumptions.
2. **Derive metrics from the use case.** Examples: customer training → adoption, renewal, expansion; education as marketing → pipeline, qualified interest, trial conversion; partner enablement → certified partners, deal registration quality (user-specific).
3. **Align distribution and monetization.** External monetization and multi-audience personalization (see other `ti-skills`) matter most for customer, partner, member, professional, and education-as-marketing motions; employee L&D may emphasize compliance, onboarding, and single-tenant patterns—validate with the customer.
4. **Ask before deciding** per `standards/conventions.md`: confirm actual audience, regions, and buying motion if the user has not specified them.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Primary use case is explicit
- [ ] Success metrics tie to that use case (not generic “completion rate” alone)
- [ ] Employee L&D is not assumed when the user described customers or partners

## **3\. Customer Problem \***

### **Objective**

What is the customer trying to achieve? Why is this important to their business?

| Sage runs learning for internal employees, partners, and customers and needs front-line managers to actively drive adoption. Managers must assign content to their learners and monitor progress without relying on centralized admins. Manager-led enablement is core to Sage’s adoption model and scaling strategy. |
| :---- |

### **Problem Statement**

“I am a manager trying to assign training and track progress for my direct learners, but I cannot scope assignments and reporting to only my learners because TI does not support native manager-to-learner relationships, which forces centralized administration and reduces accountability, follow-through, and adoption.”

|  |
| :---: |

### **Top 3 User Stories (Maximum 3\)**

1. As a manager, I want to assign and unassign training to my direct learners so that I can drive adoption without relying on a central admin.  
2. As a manager, I want reporting scoped only to my learners so that I can monitor progress without accessing unrelated data.  
3. As an admin, I want audit visibility into manager actions so that governance and compliance are maintained.

---

## **4\. Current State & Product Gap \***

### **What TI Supports Today**

TI supports assignment and reporting through centralized admin roles and grouping structures such as Panorama and sublicenses. Reporting is broad and not inherently scoped to a manager-to-learner relationship.

### **Product Gap**

TI does not support:

• Native manager → learner hierarchical relationships  
• Scoped reporting tied to those relationships  
• Assignment capabilities limited to a manager’s direct learners  
• Audit tracking of manager-level assignment actions

Panorama is not a viable substitute because:

• Requires one sublicense per manager (not scalable)  
• Creates structural conflicts between C1 internal and C2 customer Panoramas  
• Requires ongoing IT involvement via SSO/API when org structures change  
• Does not model true person-based hierarchy

### **Evidence**

Multiple enterprise customers have requested this capability. Current guidance requires use of Panorama workarounds or external reporting solutions.

---

## **5\. Impact & Opportunity \***

### **Customer Impact**

• Centralized admin bottlenecks  
• Reduced manager accountability for adoption  
• Lower learner engagement across distributed teams  
• Governance risk (overexposure vs insufficient access)  
• Inability to scale enablement across thousands of teams/accounts

### **Business Impact for TI**

• Broad enterprise demand signal  
• Impacts both internal (C1) and extended enterprise (C2) use cases  
• Competitive maturity gap vs LMS platforms that support hierarchical scoping  
• Potential unlock for enterprise expansions and higher adoption-driven retention

### **Why Now**

Enterprise customers are scaling manager-led enablement programs globally. Governance and reporting maturity expectations are increasing, especially across distributed teams and extended enterprise models.

---

## **6\. Workarounds & Limitations \***

### **Current Workarounds**

• Panorama sublicenses per manager  
• Manual centralized assignment processes  
• External reporting dashboards outside TI

### **Why Not Viable Long-Term**

• Not scalable (one sublicense per manager)  
• Requires constant IT intervention for org changes  
• Conflicts with existing Panorama structure  
• Moves oversight outside TI, reducing platform stickiness  
• Increases operational overhead

---

## **7\. Success Criteria & Measurement \***

### **Quantitative Metrics**

Metric: % of managers actively assigning training  
Baseline: Unknown  
Target: \>60% manager adoption within 90 days of rollout  
How Measured: Assignment event tracking

Metric: Reduction in admin assignment workload  
Baseline: Manual central assignment model  
Target: ≥40% reduction in admin hours spent assigning  
How Measured: Customer survey \+ workflow audit

Metric: Learner completion rates  
Baseline: Current program completion rates  
Target: \+10–15% increase in completion for manager-driven cohorts  
How Measured: Reporting analytics

### **Qualitative Success Indicators**

• Managers can self-serve training oversight without admin intervention  
• Scoped reporting prevents data overexposure  
• Clear audit trail of manager actions  
• Customers no longer rely on Panorama restructuring for hierarchy modeling

---

## **8\. Solution Direction (Optional)**

### **Desired Outcome**

Introduce a native “People Manager” role with:

• Manager-to-learner relationship modeling (hierarchy, reference field, or segment logic)  
• Manager dashboard with scoped reporting (progress, completions, due dates, engagement)  
• Scoped assignment workflow  
• Audit logging of manager actions

### **Constraints / Non-Goals**

• Must coexist with Panorama architecture  
• Must support API and SSO-based user provisioning  
• Not intended to replace Panorama entirely

---

1. Customers can provide and maintain manager-to-learner relationships via UI, SSO, or API.  
2. Managers will actively use assignment and reporting features if provided.  
3. Scoped reporting can be layered onto existing reporting infrastructure without major architectural conflict.
